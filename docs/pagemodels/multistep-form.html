Register a birth

Print

All births in Queensland must be registered within 60 days (that's 8 weeks) of the baby being born.

If you meet the eligibility requirements, you can register the birth. It's free to do and you can order and pay for a birth certificate at the same time.

It can take up to 20 minutes to complete this form.

Eligibility

We cannot register a birth if your baby was delivered before 20 weeks, or if the number of weeks is unknown and your baby weighed less than 400g. However, you can commemorate your family's loss with an early pregnancy loss recognition certificate.

What you'll need

  • 2 forms of identification.
  • baby's details including:
    • full name
    • date, time and place of birth
    • birth weight in grams.
  • details of the parent(s) including:
    • names and address
    • place and date of the birth
    • name and sex of the baby
    • places and dates of parents' birth
    • date of parents' marriage or civil partnership
    • parents' jobs
    • mother's maiden surname

Processing times

Processing of the registration form can take up to 10 business days.

If you order a birth certificate at the same time, we'll post the certificate to you as soon as the registration has been processed.

Fees

  • Free - Registrations within 60 days of the birth.
  • $5.45 - May apply for late registrations outside the 60 day period.
  • $52.60 - Official birth certificate (standard postage included).
  • $68.35 - Commemorative birth certificate package (1 official birth certificate and standard postage included).

More information

  1. Download and print the birth registration application form.
  2. Complete the registration form.
  3. Have both parents sign the declaration section of the form, and another adult (18 or older) witness your signatures.
  4. Submit your registration form to us, either
    • in person at
      • the Brisbane registry customer service centre at Level 32, 180 Ann Street, Brisbane
      • Queensland Magistrates Court or Queensland Government Agent Program (QGAP) office (except the Brisbane Magistrates Court).
    • by post to
      Registry of Births, Deaths and Marriages
      PO Box 15188
      CITY EAST QLD 4002

If you are unable to print the form, email bdm-mail@justice.qld.gov.au with your postal address.

You can pay:

  • online
    • by credit card (Visa or MasterCard)
  • at the customer service centre
    • by EFTPOS or credit card
    • with a business account cheque
    • with a bank cheque
    • with a money order
  • through the mail
    • within Australia
      • by credit card
      • with a business account cheque
      • with a bank cheque
      • with a money order
    • from overseas
      • by credit card.

We will accept cash at the customer service centre but prefer contactless payments, such as EFTPOS or credit card.

We do not accept personal cheques or cheques issued from outside Australia (in any currency).

Make Australian-drawn cheques and money orders payable to 'Registry of Births, Deaths and Marriages'.

Parents have the right to choose the name of their child. However, when considering names, the Registrar-General must refuse to register a name that:

  • is obscene or offensive
  • is too long
  • includes symbols without phonetic significance—for example, brackets (), colons (:), question marks (?), quotation marks (“), numbers, at symbols (@), plus signs (+)
  • includes or resembles an official title or rank—for example Duchess, Sergeant, Doctor, MP (Member of Parliament)
  • is a statement—for example, Save Mother Earth
  • is a political slogan
  • is otherwise contrary to the public interest.

The prohibited name policy includes detailed advice about what kinds of names are prohibited.

Found a mistake?

If you have submitted your baby's birth registration online in the last 2 days and have found a mistake, email M-BDM-Online-Birth-Registration@justice.qld.gov.au.

If you submitted it more than 2 days ago, request a review of the certificate.
